Three All-Nighters for CS

Posted on December 12, 2013

My oh my! The past three weeks. Or the past three days at least. #thestruggleisreal. I pulled three all nighters the past three days working on the last CS project CS61B. Today, at 7:02am, I submitted my project for the last time. Last CS project this year! It was an incredible journey.

It was not without challenges though. Working with my friends certainly strained some of my relationships. On the second night, I had code that was outputting text that was the correct output, but I hadn't comprehensively tested the code yet. It was 3am and I didn't have the willpower to test it, considering that I had worked for the past seven or eight hours, and also the night before. They ended up taking my code and trying to make it "their own." They didn't test the code. Fortunately, another friend Brandea ended up testing my code and discovered that we had failed all the autograder tests, save for one. Perhaps, that should be an "unfortunately." But, essentially, the guys had copied non-working code instead of testing it. I was really annoyed by them. Brandea stayed up with me to fix the bugs in the code - some were larger than the others. For example, my code at the time was capturing T: P1 P2 P3 in terms of (T): (P1 P2 P3) with the a space after the colon. But, it wasn't capturing T: alone since I was expecting a space after the colon and then something else. Anyways, that was a simple fix since it took a couple of lines. Most bugs involved isolating where it occured and fixing them. I'm internally grateful to Brandea for staying up with me to insert print statements everywhere so I could figure out where the bugs were occuring. Had she not stayed with me, I would have just given up. Hnin also provided a lot of moral support when she woke up at 5am and looked over our shoulder. The other guys, the people who copied my nonworking code, ended up going to sleep at 4. We stayed up until 8 fixing the code and managed to reduce to one bug, which I resolved in the afternoon after writing my IEOR paper and turning that and my ESPM 40 paper. Essentially, in the middle of my three all nighters for my CS project, I had to do two papers for my other classes. What a week! Last night, I finished the last part of the project, the tripfinder program. I stayed up until 7am, 4 hours past my slip hours deadline, so I lost 5/3% percent on my project. Oh well. So glad it's over. I feel like a much more experienced programmer after this project, especially after I managed to make the makefile program to work.

I'm afraid that I have been straining relationships at this point. I might be leaving no time for other people and other things because of my heavy workload. I think I've quit PiE as well. I don't think I have time for a special someone. That's what I'm a little concerned about at this point. But, in the end, I'm more excited about my upcoming trip to Asia this winter break. I really need that break. I can't wait to fly around the continent and exploring the different niches. It's going to be so awesome!

I'll post a reflection of my first semester soon. My finals week are next week and I have to do a research paper draft due Friday and also start studying for my other classes, which I haven't started. I'm feeling a little overwhelmed, but knowing I made it through the last three days lets me know that I can survive the next 7. Good night!

November 20 - 28

Posted on November 28, 2013

Well, I'm finally home for Thanksgiving. I haven't blogged in a while and so much has happened in the last week. Or, so I hope. Let's see what I can recall.

  • BearHack

    • Over the weekend was Hackers @ Berkeley's Fall 2013 BearHack. Over the course of the first day, my team gradually expanded in size. By the end of the Hackathon, our primary members were Can, Cem, Alan, Daniel, Gavin, and I. We ended up developing a hardware hack that unlocked a door. A motor was connected to a door handle and, at request, it would pull the door handle down, thereby unlocking a door. I was responsible for the front-end and back-end portion of the hack. I developed the interface for which a user would login and operate the robot and also the server, written in Python and utilizing the Flask module, to communicate with the motor.

    By the time the Hackathon ended, I had been there for 27 hours, with only 1 hour of nap. For that nap, I sprawled across the tables on the 6th floor in Soda. When I woke up, I was freezing. Anyways, our hack won the award for the Best First Hack - the "noobie" award. It was an awesome experience, really.

  • BonFire (A nonfire!)

    • On Friday, I met up with Cem and Can at the Greek Theater for the Bonfire tradition, a rally before the Big Game between our team and Stanford's. I had never been in the Greek Theater before, but I discovered that the atmosphere that it creates was truly spectacular. Unfortunately, high winds led firefighters forbid our bonfire. But, they did have some awesome pyros and tried in every way to make up for the lack of the bonfire. I really wished for it, though, as it was quite cold.

Actually, it turns out that nothing much really happened. Today, Colin, Vivian, and Andrew dropped by my house to say hi. Tomorrow, we plan to eat lunch together - hopefully, we can find a restaurant that is open! Alright, g'night.
